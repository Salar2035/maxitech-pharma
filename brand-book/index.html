02 · VoiceTone Principles
02.1

We write the way we manufacture: controlled.

Clear

Short sentences, exact words. Product names, strengths and directions are never ambiguous. Clarity is a safety feature, not a style choice.

Measured

We state what the evidence supports and stop there. No superlatives, no hype. Confidence comes from precision, not volume.

Humane

Behind every prescription is a person. When we speak about patients, warmth is allowed in: plain language, respect, and reassurance.

02 · VoiceClaims & Compliance
02.4

The words carry
a licence.

Pharmaceutical language is regulated language. These rules apply to every asset: packaging, detail aids, social posts, and anything generated from Section 08.

R1

No cure claims

Never "cures", "guaranteed", "miracle", or "100% safe". State the indication as registered; nothing more.

R2

Name, strength, form: always exact

Brand name, generic name, strength and dosage form appear exactly as registered with DRAP. No abbreviations on patient-facing material.

R3

Audience separation

Prescription products are promoted to healthcare professionals only. Consumer-facing material is limited to OTC lines.

R4

Pharmacovigilance is visible

Adverse-reaction reporting ("Report Drug Reaction") is part of the brand. Carry the reporting line on collateral where space allows.

03 · LogoThe Primary Lockup
03.1

The primary
lockup.

The mark joins two parts: the MAXITECH wordmark in bold capitals, orbited by two sweeping arcs: a sky-blue swoosh above and a deep navy swoosh below. Together they read as motion around a stable core: science in service of care.

This is the primary, first-use identity. It is never redrawn, recoloured or rebuilt, only placed, sized and given room to breathe. The wordmark's colours are fixed, anchored by Logo Royal #002B71.

Primary logo · full lockup
17A Legacy of Brilliance in Care
03 · LogoConstruction & Grid
03.2

Built on
a grid.

Set the lockup on an 8 × 8 grid. The wordmark sits on the central horizontal axis; the upper swoosh enters at the top-left quadrant and exits above the final letter; the lower swoosh mirrors it beneath. Use the grid to place and align the mark, never to redraw it.

Because the proportions are locked, never stretch, condense or rebuild the mark. Always scale it as one whole.

8 × 8 construction grid

05 · TypographyPharma Type Rules
05.4

Type that carries
a dosage.

On packs, leaflets and detail aids, hierarchy is fixed: brand name leads, generic name follows, strength stands apart. Nothing decorative competes with them.

T1

Brand → generic → strength, in that order

Brand name in Poppins Medium caps. Generic name (INN) in Inter, sentence case, always adjacent. Strength in a larger weight, never smaller than the generic.

T2

Fine print has a floor

Regulatory text never sets below 7pt in print or 11px on screen. If it doesn't fit, the layout changes, not the type size.

T3

Numerals are lining, spacing is tabular

Dosages, batch numbers and dates use tabular figures so columns align and misreads are impossible.

T4

Localisation ready

Urdu and export-market translations follow the same hierarchy. Reserve 20% extra space on any bilingual surface.
